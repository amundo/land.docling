Precursors

The history of language documentation (in a broad sense) is almost as old as writing. Consider the Sumerian-Akkadian “lexical list” — one of the oldest known bilingual dictionaries — which dates from the third miliennium B.C.E..

It’s not a coincidence that this ancient clay tablet has a lot in common with a modern spreadsheet. There is a clear column division from top to bottom: the left column contains Sumerian terms, and the right column an Akkadian translation. Thus, rows correspond to “words”, and columns correspond to languages.

Digitally capturing those data structures enables the design of digital documents and interactive user interfaces.
